A Devoted Son

- Anita Desai

Summary

The story 'A Devoted Son' had been written by an Indian writer Anita Desai. It's a story based on the overall family activities of Mr. Verma. It's a story about Mr. Verma's family in the rural village of India.

The story's plot develops with the news that Rakesh topped the medical exam which made his father very much glad. Being very happy and satisfied with his son's result Mr. Verma organized a party in house where well wishers were fed with delicious items, sweetmeats bettel-leaves, etc along with the best wishes of them to Rakesh slapping on his back. Then, Rakesh went to USA for further study. He worked there in famous of prestigious hospitals in USA. Rakesh as well as his family members were missing each other very much. Finally after some time, Rakesh returned to his hometown and married a simple but not well educated girl which was chosen by his mother. But his father was dissatisfied as he wanted his son to marry a well educated girl. Rakesh continued his task & worked in city hospital. He soon became a clinic owner too. Along with the time elapsed, his father and mother were becoming old day by day. In the mean time, Rakesh's mother died but in a cherished way as she got all the care. wanted from her son. He convinces his father. Also, Rakesh got a child.

Now, Verma's health condition also goes on deteriorating day by day. His body couldn't digest all the foods. So, Rakesh himself being a doctor banned some sweet, spicy and oily food to his father and he forbade his wife to feed his father rich foods. Rakesh being a devoted son, every morning he used to read newspapers for his father, he used to take him in open space for fresh air. He used to serve him a cup of tea not in ordinary cup chinese but in a brass tumbler. Besides, all these carings Verma was always disappointed with his son's behaviour towards him. The only thing Verma was disappointed with was that he wasn't allowed to eat sweet and oily foods which he liked very much. He had his one old friend Vatia with whom he shares his personal experiences One day Mr. Verma bribed his grandson to buy him a jalebi for thirty paisa and he gave twenty paisa for him. But unfortunately the conspirator caught reed handed and Rakesh shouted at his father. Verma continued to hate his son but Rakesh took care of his father very much. Verma being very much dissatisfied announced in front of his son that he wanted no more medicine rather he was waiting for death. But Rakesh never thought badly, but he remained devoted until his last breath. He wanted to show sympathy, love and care which his father used to do when he was a child. At last story ended with Verma's death.

Understanding the Text

a. How did the morning papers bring ambience of celebration in the Verma family?

The morning papers brought ambience of celebration in the Verma family with the joyous news of achievement of Rakesh in his academic life where Rakesh had topped the examination.

b. How did the community celebrate Rakeshi success?

The community celebrated Rakesh's success in a marvellous way. As the community knew about Rakesh's success, they arrived to Rakesh's house and congratulated Rakesh as well his family members. They celebrated as if it was festival with sound and colours.

c. Why was Rakesh's success a special matter of discussion in the neighbourhood?

Rakesh's success was a special matter of discussion in the neighbourhood because besides his family's economic background Rakesh topped the examination.

d. How does the author make fun with the words 'America' and 'the USA'?

The author makes fun with the words 'America" and "the USA" by presenting the opinion of Verma regarding the words. The author has presented fun through Verma's opinions saying that he has never called the country's name 'America'. For him, ignorant neighbours only say America.

e. How does author characterize Rakesh's wife?

The author characterizes Rakesh's wife as a simple person. She is well mannered, but she is illiterate. She respects her husband and family very much.

f. How does Rakesh rise in his career?

Rakesh is quite hardworking and a brilliant student since his childhood. Besides his family's background, he tops the examination. In this way he goes to America and works in a prestigious hospital. He returns to his hometown and works in a city hospital. No sooner, he becomes his own clinic owner. In this way with his hardwork he becomes the richest doctor in the town.

g. How does the author describe Rakesh's family background?

The author describes Rakesh's family background showing the miserable condition of his family. Rakesh belongs to very poor and illiterate family background. His grandparents used to do the business of vegetable vendors. His father has worked so hard for forty years in kerosene depot. They have a small yellow house at the end of the road.

h. What is the impact of Rakesh's mother's death on his father?

There is quite a bad impact of Rakesh's mother's death in his father. His father felt quite sad and body started recutting badly. His acts are quite uncommon. He spits his red bettle juice from his mouth anywhere. He was like as if he tries to mock his family member's behaviour.

i. What did Rakesh do to make his father's old age more comfortable?

Rakesh tried with his all efforts to make his fathers old age more comfortable for his fathers convenience, he used to serve him tea early in the morning, read newspaper and he forbade foods that deteriorate his father health.

j. Why did the old man try to bribe his grandson?

The old man tried to bribe his grandson in order to fulfill his desires for sweets. He wanted sweet jalebis because he had not eaten jalebis for long as he was forbidden to eat jalebis.

k. Are Mr. Verma's complains about his diet undesirable? How?

Ms. Verma's complains about his diet are somehow reasonable to some extent from his context as he was not allowed to eat sweet and oily foods.

But in my context, his complains about his diet are unreasonable because his own son was a doctor and forbidding him to eat sweet and oily food was all in favour of his health.

Reference to the Context

a. How did Verma couple make sacrifices for their son's higher education?

Verma couple made sacrifices for their son's higher education by doing hard labour in their entire lifetime. To achieve their dream of educating son, Verma worked hard for forty years in kerosene depot whereas his wife worked in household tasks.

b. Mr. Varma suffers from diseases one after another after his wife's death. Would he have enjoyed better health if she had not died before him? Give reasons.

Yes, he would have enjoyed better health if his wife had not died before him. Mr. Varma started to feel weak only after his wife's death. As he loses her, he found himself alone and suffered from depression. He started doing unusual activities. He felt broken into pieces.

c. Dr. Rakesh is divided between a doctor and a son. As he loves his father and worries about his weakening health, but as a doctor, he is strict on father's diet and medicine. In your view, what else could Rakesh have done to make his father's final years more comfortable?

In my opinion, Rakesh could have done the following things to make his father's final year more comfortable:

a. He could have treated his father more friendly instead of shouting at him.
b. He could have tried to understand his fathers feelings instead of becoming tensed about his health.
c. He could have provided his father with mental support.
d. He could have brought a bit of change in himself instead of changing his father.
e. He could have allowed only little sweet foods as his father's demand.

d. What does story say about relationship between grandfather and grandson?

In some families we find bond relationships between grandparents and grandson. In this story too a relationship between grandfather and grandson is presented · From this relation we can assure that a person loves interest more than his principal. The grandson is one and only person with whom Verma spends most of the time. He starts to fulfill his desires through grandson. But, here we find that Verma is seeking benefits from an innocent child who know nothing about bribe.

e. Do you call Rakesh a devoted son?

From Verma's point of view, Rakesh may not be a devoted son but from my point of view Rakesh is a devoted son. Caring his father, every morning he serves him a cup of tea, not in ordinary china glass but in Varma's favourite brass tumbler, he reads newspaper everyday, he manages him in the garden. Besides all these activities the main thing is that caring for his father's health he forbade some spices of foods. Also, he kept on serving his father though his father used to curse him through his thorny words.

Reference Beyond the Text

Family refers to a wide network of extended relationships. Parents have great ambition for their children in Nepali Society. Most of the parents of the children are predetermined and preoccupied with the prejudices that their children to be the best person in the society such as the best doctor, the best engineer, the best teacher, and many more. Some instances provoke such ideas:

a. Due to parents’ ambition for their children, some children labour hard to achieve their destination and got succeed in the end.
b. Parents are the facilitator and guides who can lead their children to the right path of success and help them to obtain achievement in life.
c. Every parent wishes their child to become the brightest star in the group. In some cases, their pushing to their kids leads them to succeed and help them to be better in life.
d. Sometimes, parent’s determining the future of their children do not prove how good their parents are and it leads destruction of life as well. Thus, parents should listen to the voice and aim of their children as well for their better in future.
e. Anyway, the duty of parents is to stay close to their children, spend time together, play and relax which will provide them with a sense of security and build a positive bond for their future.

b. Medicines replace our diets in old age. What can be done to make old ageless dependent on medicine?

Medicines replace our diets in old age. With age, the number of calories you need begins to decline. “As we get older, the body becomes less efficient at absorbing some key nutrients,” says Katherine Tucker. In addition, the ability to taste food declines, blunting appetite. Some foods become difficult to chew or digest. Thus, medicines become the best option to replace our diets in old age. To make old ageless dependent on medicine, they should do the following things:

a. Eat plenty of colourful fruits and vegetables such as fruits like pineapples, oranges and vegetables such as carrots, pumpkin, and sweet potatoes.
b. Eat cereals, bread, crackers, rice or pasta every day mostly high fibre varieties.
c. Have low-fat or fat-free dairy (milk, or cheese) that are fortified with vitamin D to help keep your bones healthy.
d. Use of mustard oil or olive oil as a cooking medium as both prevent high cholesterol and improve good cholesterol in the blood.
e. Egg whites are a good source of protein to repair worn-out cells and tissues.
f. Vegetable soups without cream and thickening agents are a healthy meal to be taken as supper.
g. Drink about 10-15 glasses of water a day to prevent dehydration especially in summer.
h. Also, exercise regularly, stretch and do yoga to ensure absorption of nutrients well.
i. Balancing physical activity and a healthful diet is the best recipe for health and fitness.
j. Set a goal to be physically active for at least 30 minutes every day.
